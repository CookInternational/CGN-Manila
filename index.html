Isabel Reyes

Manila Bureau Chief

Beats: Bureau coverage

Isabel Reyes is a CGN News Foreign Correspondent stationed in Manila, covering the Philippines, Southeast Asia, maritime security, democratic institutions, regional diplomacy, climate risk, consumer pressure and the business corridors linking Manila to Japan, the United States and the wider Indo-Pacific. Her reporting focuses on how politics, sea-lane security, infrastructure, energy costs and typhoon resilience shape daily life across the archipelago and the region.

Maya Reyes

Local/Civic Reporter

Beats: Local / civic affairs

Maya Reyes was born in Quezon City and studied journalism and public administration at the University of the Philippines Diliman. She covers city government, transport, public services and civic life for CGN Manila, with a focus on how decisions about roads, rail, schools, permits and neighbourhood services affect families across Metro Manila. Reyes speaks English and Filipino, keeps a rescue aspin named Taho, and says her best reporting often starts with one commuter asking why something simple has become so hard.

Daniel Cruz

Politics Reporter

Beats: Politics

Daniel Cruz was born in Pasig and studied political science and public policy at Ateneo de Manila University. He covers government, elections, policy and public institutions for CGN Manila, following the national and local decisions that shape democratic life, public spending, security debates and voter confidence. Cruz speaks English and Filipino, keeps a well-worn copy of the Constitution near his desk, and is known in the newsroom for turning long committee hearings into clear stakes for ordinary readers.
